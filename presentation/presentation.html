Dimension Reduction

Current thoughts, future directions

Curse of dimensionality

Can't visualise

Nothing is average

Distances get out of perspective

  • In two dimensions, confusing density and mass isn't too bad
  • In higher dimensions, volume grows ~rN

Eventually, almost everything ends up on the shell of a 'sphere' with nothing in the middle

And on that shell, everything is equidistant from everything else

Remove noisy dimensions

Do something computationally easy to get rid of the worst noise

  • Most informative genes
  • Most consistent patterns of behaviour
  • ⇒ PCA

PCA is linear

Want to capture as many components as there are linearly distinct processes

  • Increase weights of interesting genes
  • Kernel-PCA
  • Supervised PCA

tSNE and its ilk

  • Find a 2 dimensional surface in the PCA space that preserves neighbourliness
  • Spread cells randomly in 2D and move them around until similarity in distribution of distances is found
  • Crowding problem - there's not as much space in 2D.
  • It's an exploratory tool - not a complete picture
  • Can't add extra samples/cells
  • Can add extra info - sample_id, spatial information,...
  • Can use one run to 'seed' another

Alternatives

UMAP should capture more of the global/topological nature of the data. Fast and allows for prediction

Phate emphasises diffusion through states
